Classify each numbered item into exactly one knowledge category.

Categories:
- fact: A specific, verifiable data point or business rule.
- definition: Explains WHAT something IS — defines a term, role, or entity. Answers 'what is X?'.
- how-to: Step-by-step process, procedure, or recipe. Answers 'how do I do X?
- reference: Describes how a system, feature, or specification WORKS — its structure, rules, configuration, or operational details. Answers 'how does X work?'
- concept: An abstract principle, mental model, or framework for understanding.
- insight: A personal conclusion, learned lesson, or practical recommendation.

KEY DISTINCTION: 'definition' answers 'what IS this thing?' while 'reference' answers 'how does this thing WORK?' If the text describes system behavior, feature specs, rules, or configuration details, choose 'reference' not 'definition'.
